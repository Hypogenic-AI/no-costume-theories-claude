# Datasets for "No Costume Theories" Research

This is a theoretical/philosophical research project. The primary "datasets" are structured databases of empirical studies and their theoretical interpretations, rather than raw experimental data.

## Dataset 1: ConTraSt Database (Consciousness Theories Studies)

### Overview
- **Source**: https://contrastdb.tau.ac.il/
- **Publication**: Yaron et al. (2022), Nature Human Behaviour, Vol. 6
- **Size**: 412 experiments from published studies
- **Format**: Web-based interactive database (no bulk download available)
- **Task**: Meta-analysis of how empirical studies relate to consciousness theories
- **License**: Open access

### Description
The ConTraSt database catalogs empirical studies that interpret findings in light of at least one of four leading neuroscientific theories: GNW, HOT, IIT, or RPT. Studies are classified by experimental paradigms, stimuli, neuroscientific techniques, empirical findings, and theoretical interpretations.

**Key finding**: Supporting a specific theory can be predicted solely from methodological choices, irrespective of findings. Most studies interpret findings post hoc rather than testing critical predictions a priori.

### Access Instructions
1. Visit https://contrastdb.tau.ac.il/
2. Use the web interface to filter and explore experiments
3. For programmatic access, the bioRxiv preprint describes the data schema

### Relevance to Our Research
This database directly demonstrates the "no costume theories" problem: if theory allegiance is predicted by methodology rather than findings, the distinction between theories may be more "aesthetic" (choice of methods/framing) than empirically grounded.

## Dataset 2: ARC-Cogitate Experimental Data

### Overview
- **Source**: https://osf.io/mbcfy/
- **Size**: fMRI, MEG, iEEG data from 256 participants
- **Format**: Neuroimaging data (BIDS format)
- **Task**: Adversarial collaboration testing IIT vs GNW predictions
- **License**: Open access (via OSF)

### Description
The ARC-Cogitate project provides the first large-scale adversarial collaboration data between IIT and GNW theories. Published in Nature (2025), the results showed neither theory's key predictions were fully supported.

### Access Instructions
```bash
# Data available at OSF
# Visit: https://osf.io/mbcfy/

```

### Relevance to Our Research
This dataset provides the most rigorous empirical test to date of whether competing consciousness theories make behaviorally distinguishable predictions - a central question for our "no costume theories" hypothesis.
