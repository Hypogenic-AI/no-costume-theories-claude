# Downloaded Papers

## Foundational Philosophy Papers

1. **Quining Qualia** - `dennett_1988_quining_qualia.pdf`
   - Authors: Daniel Dennett
   - Year: 1988
   - Source: Consciousness in Modern Science, Oxford University Press
   - Why relevant: Argues qualia are incoherent; behavioral/functional properties exhaust what matters. Directly supports the "no costume theories" position that representational distinctions without behavioral consequences are empty.

2. **Zombies and the Explanatory Gap** - `chalmers_zombies_explanatory_gap.pdf`
   - Authors: David Chalmers
   - Year: 2018 (from Norton Introduction to Philosophy)
   - Source: Book chapter
   - Why relevant: Presents the opposing view - that consciousness involves something beyond functional/behavioral properties. The zombie argument is the strongest challenge to behavioral testability requirements.

3. **On a Confusion about a Function of Consciousness** - `block_1997_access_consciousness.pdf`
   - Authors: Ned Block (1995, with continuing commentary 1997)
   - Source: Behavioral and Brain Sciences
   - Why relevant: Introduces the P-consciousness vs A-consciousness distinction - a paradigm case of representational distinction (phenomenal vs access) that may lack clear behavioral tests.

4. **Chalmers' Zombie Argument** - `kind_chalmers_zombie_argument.pdf`
   - Authors: Amy Kind
   - Source: Philosophy chapter
   - Why relevant: Analysis of the zombie argument and its implications for behavioral testability of consciousness.

5. **Two Kinds of Consciousness** - `burge_1997_two_kinds_consciousness.pdf`
   - Authors: Tyler Burge
   - Year: 1997
   - Source: UCLA Philosophy
   - Why relevant: Alternative framework for distinguishing types of consciousness.

## Representational and Higher-Order Theories

6. **Empirical Support for Higher-Order Theories** - `lau_rosenthal_2011_empirical_HOT.pdf`
   - Authors: Hakwan Lau & David Rosenthal
   - Year: 2011
   - Source: Trends in Cognitive Sciences
   - Why relevant: Demonstrates that HOT makes distinct behavioral predictions (awareness without performance, performance without awareness), showing this theory IS behaviorally testable.

7. **Phenomenal Consciousness, Attention and Accessibility** - `schlicht_phenomenal_consciousness_access.pdf`
   - Authors: Tobias Schlicht
   - Source: PhilPapers
   - Why relevant: Examines whether phenomenal consciousness can be separated from access/behavioral markers.

## Methodology and Testability

8. **Quantifying Empirical Support for Theories of Consciousness** - `wiese_2024_quantifying_empirical_support.pdf`
   - Authors: Asger Kirkeby-Hinrup
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: Frontiers in Psychology
   - Why relevant: Proposes Bayesian framework for comparing theories based on empirical support. Directly addresses how to evaluate which theories have genuine behavioral/empirical grounding.

9. **Adversarial Collaboration Protocol (COGITATE)** - `cogitate_2023_adversarial_protocol.pdf`
   - Authors: Melloni, Mudrik, Pitts et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: PLOS ONE
   - Why relevant: The gold-standard methodology for testing whether consciousness theories make distinguishable empirical predictions. Key test case for whether IIT and GNW are behaviorally differentiable.

10. **ConTraSt Database** - `contrast_database_2021.pdf`
    - Authors: Yaron, Melloni et al.
    - Year: 2021
    - Source: bioRxiv (later Nature Human Behaviour 2022)
    - Why relevant: Meta-analysis showing theory allegiance is predicted by methodology, not findings - evidence that theories may be "costume" versions of each other.

11. **Conscious Perception and the Prefrontal Cortex** - `michel_conscious_perception_pfc.pdf`
    - Authors: Matthias Michel
    - Source: PhilArchive
    - Why relevant: Examines contrastive methodology and behavioral markers of consciousness.

## AI and Consciousness Assessment

12. **Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence** - `butlin_2023_consciousness_in_ai.pdf`
    - Authors: Butlin, Long, Elmoznino, Bengio et al.
    - Year: 2023
    - Source: arXiv:2308.08708
    - Why relevant: Proposes "indicator properties" derived from consciousness theories. Explicitly argues behavioral tests alone are unreliable and a theory-heavy approach is needed - directly relevant to our hypothesis.
